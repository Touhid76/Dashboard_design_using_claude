§08 — Actionable Recommendations
Rec 01 · At-Risk Intervention
🚨 Early Probation Alert System
25% of students have probation history with a CGPA 0.37 points lower than peers. Deploy an early-warning dashboard for advisors to flag students at-risk before probation occurs — targeting attendance below 75% and CGPA below 2.5.
Rec 02 · Attendance Policy
📋 Reinforce the 80% Attendance Rule
Students with 60–70% attendance have the lowest CGPA (2.98). Suspended students average 4.3% lower attendance. Strict enforcement combined with flexible make-up classes can bridge this performance gap significantly.
Rec 03 · Study Quality
📚 Promote Structured Study Routines
Students with 5+ daily study hours show higher CGPA. Introduce guided study halls and 2–3 session/day scheduling workshops. Academic planners and peer-led study groups can reinforce this habit across the student body.
Rec 04 · Digital Wellbeing
📱 Digital Balance Program
Social media usage (avg 3.3h) now exceeds study hours. Heavy users (10h+) study ~1hr less. Launch a digital wellness workshop series teaching time-blocking and app-limit strategies to redirect screen time toward academics.
Rec 05 · Holistic Development
🏆 Encourage Balanced Engagement
Co-curricular participants maintain equal CGPA (3.28 vs 3.27), proving engagement doesn't hurt academics. Expand club programs and skill development tracks — 3 hrs/day of skill building correlates with peak CGPA (3.34).
Rec 06 · Equity & Access
🌱 Income-Neutral Merit Programs
CGPA is consistent across income brackets (~3.27 at all levels). This signals merit-based scholarships are working. Expand financial literacy and scholarship awareness programs to ensure low-income students fully access available support.
